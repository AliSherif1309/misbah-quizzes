"Kuwaiti Traditions – Al‑Noon & Al‑Qarqiaan"

Teacher: Mr. Ali Misbah

⏱️ Time limit: 20 minutes


📖 Read & Answer
🎯 Mr. Ali Misbah

Kuwaiti society is known for the strong relations among its members. Families, neighbours and friends keep close relationships, and everyone is always ready to share and celebrate special occasions with others. There are many occasions, which became Kuwaiti customs and traditions.

"Al-Noon" is a traditional Kuwaiti occasions. When a child has the first teeth or takes the first steps, children of the family, relatives and neighbours are usually invited in the afternoon. The mother spreads a carpet in the courtyard of the house, and goes up to the housetop and then, she throws candy and nuts on it. Children collect the candy and the nuts in their clothes. The mother also presents "Al-Noon" dish to other mothers.

"Al-Qarqiaan" is another Kuwaiti occasion. In the thirteenth, fourteenth and fifteenth nights of Ramadan; as in the past, children wear traditional clothes and wander through the streets in their neighbourhood while singing. Mothers give them candy and nuts.

Due to the change in Kuwaiti life, old jobs and activities like pearl diving, fishing, and shipbuilding have disappeared. Accordingly, many celebrations and occasions, such as traditional songs, dances and traditional activities disappeared.
